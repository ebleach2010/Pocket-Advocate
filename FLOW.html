The offer

Advocacy Case

$100

A live discussion with you covering their symptoms, labs, and medical journey. A private case file holds their uploads, the recording, and a live chat with you. Within one week of the call, they receive a comprehensive written report and flow chart: what was discussed, next steps, and anything you found afterward.

Follow-up add-on

+$50

Offered only at checkout. Adds a second schedulable discussion to the same case, bookable any time after the report lands. Skip it, and a later follow-up is a fresh $100 case with the full benefits — no discounted retro add-on.

Pocket Advocate

$20/mo

Round-the-clock access to the chat with you, with your live online status. You reply on your own time, when you're available — the terms say plainly that response timing is never guaranteed, and that's the trade the subscriber accepts. Separate from cases. Cancel anytime.

Everywhere, in writing: this is advocacy, not medicine. No diagnosis, no treatment, no prescriptions, not a doctor-patient relationship, not a substitute for one, not emergency care. This disclaimer appears at signup, on every case page, and in the footer of every report.

The client's journey

Before any money moves
  1. 1

    Landing page

    The three products, plain-language pricing, what this is and is not, and the pitch for public sessions in your voice.

  2. 2

    Create an account

    Email magic-link sign-in. No passwords for anyone to forget or for you to support.

  3. 3

    Read and acknowledge the waivers

    Service disclaimer, privacy & data handling, and recording consent — one per screen, scroll to the end, explicitly acknowledge each.

  4. 4

    Public or private?

    Its own screen, never bundled into the purchase. Private is pre-selected. Public consent can be revoked any time until the broadcast starts.

"Other patients gain insight into their medical journey when live discussion of cases are presented. However, this is entirely optional depending on your privacy preferences."
Your framing, verbatim, on the election screen. Public = the call is broadcast live on the TheBroScientist YouTube channel.
Book & pay — one flow
  1. 5

    Pick a time and a meeting method

    Your availability calendar. Slots must be at least 72 hours out, between 8am and 6pm MST — shown alongside their local time.

    Discord voice channel · PreferredZoom callPhone call

    Discord is the default and badged preferred — they can stream their own camera there, so it works like any video meeting.

  2. 6

    Pay at the moment of scheduling

    Stripe Checkout: $100, plus the optional $50 follow-up add-on as a line item. The slot is held for ~15 minutes while they pay; abandon checkout and it frees up.

  3. 7

    The case opens — on both sides

    Payment confirmation creates the case with the appointment attached. A case can never exist without a payment. The live chat unlocks now, with your online status visible.

Before the call
  1. 8

    They upload, they finish forms

    Labs, imaging, and records go into the case file. The appointment stays "pending" until every required form is acknowledged — then it flips to confirmed. Reminders go out at 48 and 24 hours if forms are unfinished.

The call
  1. 9

    The discussion

    On the channel they chose — the case page shows the Discord or Zoom link, or the number to expect for a phone call. Public sessions stream live to your YouTube channel; private sessions stay between you.

Afterward
  1. 10

    You upload the recording

    It lands in their case file.

  2. 11

    Within 7 days: the report

    You upload the comprehensive report/flow chart; they get pinged instantly. Your admin view counts down the days so the deadline never silently slips.

  3. 12

    The follow-up, if they bought it

    With the $50 add-on, they book their second discussion any time after the report. Without it, the case page offers a follow-up as a fresh $100 case.

Closing
  1. ✓

    You close the case

    Once your obligations are fulfilled, one button closes it. The chat ends — but they keep access to their file forever, and every document (report, forms, recording) can be downloaded and printed.

Pocket Advocate, side by side

The subscription runs parallel to everything above and touches none of it.

Account → subscription terms (the no-guarantee clause up front, in plain words) → Stripe subscription checkout → the chat unlocks immediately with your online status. Renewals, failed payments, and cancellations are handled automatically; access runs to the end of the last paid period. To protect your time, the chat shows an expectation line you control ("Eric typically replies within a few days"), and you can pause the whole thing from your side.

The rules the system enforces

Lead time
Bookings must be 72+ hours out. Enforced by the server, not the browser.
Hours
8am–6pm MST, always anchored to Mountain time; clients see their local equivalent next to it.
Payment
At scheduling. No pay, no case, no exceptions — the case is created by the payment confirmation itself.
Consent
Private is the default. Public consent is separate, explicit, and revocable until the broadcast starts. Revoking it just makes the session private — nothing else changes.
Refunds
Needs your call: proposed — full refund if canceled 72+ hours before the slot; inside 72 hours, one free reschedule, no cash refund.
No-shows
Needs your call: proposed — client no-show forfeits after 15 minutes with one free reschedule; a no-show on your side is always a full refund or priority reschedule, their choice.

The legal picture, honestly

"HIPAA doesn't apply" is true — you're not a covered entity — but it isn't the end of the story. The FTC's Health Breach Notification Rule and state laws like Washington's My Health My Data Act reach non-HIPAA health services. Storing strangers' labs and imaging means real obligations either way. Serving the US only keeps Canadian privacy law out of scope entirely — the terms will say the service is offered to US residents only.

The app is designed for that from day one: encrypted storage, access locked to the client and you, an honest privacy notice, and strict security rules — never an open test mode. The advocacy framing ("questions to bring to your doctor," never "diagnosis" or "treatment plan") runs through every screen and the report template, because that wording is what keeps this on the right side of practicing medicine without a license.

One non-negotiable: a real lawyer reviews the waiver, privacy notice, recording consent, and subscription terms before the first paying client. Everything here is structured to make that review cheap and fast.

  3. Domain and public name — "TheBroScientist Neuro Advocacy" with "Pocket Advocate" as the subscription's name?
